I have a degree in Psychology from the National University of Córdoba. I am doing a PhD in Psychology at the Faculty of Psychology of the National University of Córdoba with a Doctoral Scholarship granted by the National Council of Scientific and Technical Research (CONICET). Currently I work as Assistant Professor in the Chair of Descriptive and Inferential Psychostatistics of the Faculty of Psychology, National University of Córdoba. I am a researcher of the KuskaRuway Team - Research in Psychology and Behavioral Economics. My main topics of interest are: Social Neuroscience, Behavioral Economics, Social Psychology and Applied Psychostatistics. My research work focuses on investigating the neurophysiological, emotional and behavioral processes that underlie the effect of Social Status in Cooperative and / or Social Decisions. The research projects in which I currently participate are related to the following topics: -The role of social status and the type of cognitive processing in the neuronal response, emotional and behavioral responses associated with cooperative decision-making.-Poverty urban and environmental problems in the city of Cordoba: Approach from psychology and behavioral economics. - Contributions of Psychology and the Behavioral Economy to the reduction of poverty and inequality.
